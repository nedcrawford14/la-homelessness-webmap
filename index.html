Homelessness in Los Angeles County

A StoryMap examining housing pressure, homelessness, and spatial inequality across Los Angeles County.

Methodology

This project analyzes homelessness in Los Angeles County through a spatial framework that connects housing affordability, population-normalized homelessness rates, and public reporting patterns.

Homelessness data were normalized by population at the census tract level to allow for meaningful comparisons across areas of varying density. Housing cost burden was calculated using American Community Survey data, while 311 service requests were used to generate a kernel density surface representing the spatial concentration of encampment-related calls.

All data processing and spatial analysis were conducted in ArcGIS Pro, with final visualization and dissemination completed through ArcGIS Online and StoryMaps.
